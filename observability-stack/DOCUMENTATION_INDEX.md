## Document Types Explained

### Final Reports (FINAL_*.md)
Authoritative, comprehensive documents that represent the final state of an analysis or assessment. These are the primary reference documents.

### Certification Documents (*_CERTIFICATION.md)
Documents that certify the system meets specific requirements for production deployment, security, or other criteria.

### Readiness Documents (*_READINESS_*.md)
Documents that assess whether the system is ready for a specific milestone (deployment, upgrade, etc.).

### Coverage Documents (*_COVERAGE_*.md)
Documents that analyze the completeness of testing, metrics, security controls, or other coverage areas.

### Quick References (QUICKREF.md, *_QUICK_*.md)
Condensed reference documents for rapid lookup of common operations, commands, or procedures.

### Implementation Documents (*_IMPLEMENTATION_*.md)
Documents describing how features, systems, or processes are implemented.

### Summary Documents (*_SUMMARY.md)
Condensed versions of larger documents, providing executive summaries or high-level overviews.

### Index Documents (*_INDEX.md)
Navigation documents that organize and categorize related documentation.
